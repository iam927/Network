WiFi AP Troubleshooting Guide

Best practices for deploying and managing Wireless Access Points (APs).

📡 AP Placement

  • Mount APs on ceilings or high walls for clear line-of-sight.
  • Keep away from metal structures and water sources (e.g., large pipes).
  • Avoid hiding APs behind thick concrete or brick walls.
  • Ensure about 15-20% overlap between adjacent AP coverage areas for smooth roaming.

📊 Channel Overlap

  • Co-Channel Interference (CCI) occurs when APs share the same channel.
  • For 2.4GHz, ONLY use non-overlapping channels: 1, 6, and 11.
  • Use a staggered channel plan (honeycomb pattern) so no two adjacent APs are on the same channel.
  • In dense areas, lower transmit power to reduce interference cells.

📶 2.4GHz vs 5GHz

Feature 2.4 GHz 5 GHz
Coverage Range Longer (penetrates objects better) Shorter
Speed / Bandwidth Lower Higher
Interference High (Bluetooth, Microwaves) Low

Common Troubleshooting Steps

If users report slow speeds or dropping connections:

  1. Verify the user is connected to the correct SSID and band.
  2. Check the AP's physical link (PoE, ethernet cable integrity).
  3. Perform an RF scan to check for new sources of interference.
  4. Ensure the AP firmware is up to date.
  5. Check DHCP scope to ensure IP addresses aren't exhausted.
